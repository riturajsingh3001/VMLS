Legal Aid Cell Orientation Program

The Legal Aid Cell of Vinayaka Missions Law School organized an orientation program titled “Free Legal Aid – Role of Law Students,” which proved to be a resounding success. This report provides an overview of the event, highlighting the guest speaker’s contributions, student participation, and the support received from faculty and the Dean.

VMLS | 20 minutes ago | 1 min read

The primary objective of the camp was to empower the Irular Tribes community through education, focusing on the importance of education, creating awareness about the POCSO Act, and enlightening them about the detrimental effects of early child marriage.

Event Overview:

The orientation program focused on the role of law students in providing free legal aid to society. It provided an opportunity for students to gain valuable insights and knowledge about their potential contributions in this important area. The program witnessed an exceptional level of enthusiasm and active engagement from our law students, demonstrating their eagerness to learn and make a positive impact. The program truly emphasized the crucial role that law students can play in promoting free legal aid. The event served as a platform to raise awareness about the significance of offering legal assistance to marginalized individuals and communities who often lack access to legal resources. It encouraged students to harness their legal knowledge and skills to contribute positively to society by providing pro bono services and supporting those in need.

We extend our deepest appreciation to Mr. V. Sundharesan, our esteemed guest speaker, for gracing the event with his presence and sharing valuable insights. His contributions left a lasting impact on the participants, inspiring them to become proactive agents of change in society through their legal skills and knowledge.

We extend a special note of gratitude to Dean Dr. Ananth Padmanaban for his unwavering support throughout the planning and execution of the orientation program. His guidance, encouragement, and visionary leadership played a pivotal role in making this event a resounding success.

Conclusion:

The “Free Legal Aid – Role of Law Students” orientation program organized by the Legal Aid Cell of Vinayaka Missions Law School successfully engaged students and emphasized their potential to make a positive impact through free legal aid. We extend our sincere appreciation to Mr. V. Sundharesan for his valuable contributions, as well as to Mr. Krishna Kishore and Dean Dr. Ananth Padmanaban for their leadership and support.
